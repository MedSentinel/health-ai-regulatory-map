# ISO/IEC 42001

Version: `v0.1`

## Why this matters

ISO/IEC 42001 is often discussed as an AI management system standard.

For MedSentinel purposes, it is best understood as part of governance language, not as a shortcut to trust.

## What it helps with conceptually

It encourages organizations to think in a structured way about:

- governance
- accountability
- oversight
- documentation
- lifecycle management

## Why it matters for health AI

Health AI companies increasingly use governance language to signal seriousness.

That makes it important to distinguish:

- having management system language
- having product-specific evidence

Those are not the same thing.

## What to look for in public materials

Public signals that may reflect real governance maturity include:

- clear accountability structure
- lifecycle language tied to real product operations
- documentation discipline
- change management language
- transparency about limits and oversight

These are still weaker than product-level validation, but better than vague seriousness signaling.

## Common mistake

Treating AI management system language as if it answers product-level safety, clinical validation, or regulatory questions.

It does not.

## What this standard does not tell you by itself

It does not tell you:

- whether the model works clinically
- whether the evidence is strong
- whether users are likely to over-rely on it
- whether the deployment setting is safe
- whether patient harm pathways are understood

## Good editorial questions

1. Is governance language being used instead of evidence language?
2. Does the company describe management structure more clearly than product performance?
3. Are process claims outrunning product claims in a way that creates false reassurance?

## Especially relevant for

- enterprise health AI vendors
- platform companies
- health AI firms emphasizing trust and governance branding
