# Clinical Decision Support Boundary

Version: `v0.1`

## Why this boundary matters

Clinical decision support sounds modest.

In practice, many systems described that way can range from:

- simple retrieval
- workflow organization
- ranked suggestions
- directional nudging
- action-shaping outputs

That means the label alone is rarely enough.

## Questions that matter

1. Is the system surfacing information or recommending a next step?
2. Can the user meaningfully challenge the output?
3. Does the system shape timing, prioritization, escalation, or routing?
4. Is the human review point real, or mostly symbolic?
5. Are public claims narrower than practical influence, or broader?

## Why this matters for regulation-aware analysis

Systems can remain formally described as support tools while still exerting meaningful influence over care or workflow.

That is one reason clinical decision support is such a persistent boundary problem in health AI.

## MedSentinel lens

The question is not only whether the system gives the final answer.

The question is how much it shapes the path to the answer.
