# Medical Advice Boundary

Version: `v0.1`

## Why this boundary matters

Patient-facing AI often sits in an unstable zone between:

- general health information
- educational guidance
- triage-style support
- individualized advice

This boundary matters because the user experience of advice can emerge before the system explicitly claims to provide medical advice.

## Questions that matter

1. Is the output generic, or tailored to a specific person’s symptoms, context, or risk?
2. Is the system guiding action, reassurance, urgency, or self-management?
3. Would a reasonable user interpret the response as advice?
4. Does the system create a false sense of authority or safety?
5. Are disclaimers doing too much of the safety work?

## Why this matters for health AI

The medical advice boundary is not only a legal or regulatory issue.
It is also:

- a safety issue
- a trust issue
- a UX issue
- an evaluation issue

## MedSentinel lens

The important question is not simply whether a company says "this is not medical advice."

It is whether the system behaves in a way users will experience as medical guidance.
