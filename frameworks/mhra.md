# MHRA

Version: `v0.1`

## Why the MHRA matters here

The MHRA is central to UK-facing discussions of AI-enabled medical technologies, especially where product claims, safety expectations, and regulatory experimentation begin to intersect.

## What this document is for

This is a compact orientation note for the UK regulatory lens on health AI.

It is not a substitute for formal regulatory guidance.

## Key MHRA-oriented questions

1. What kind of health technology is actually being described?
2. Is the AI component part of a medical device claim?
3. Is the product being discussed as innovation, sandbox experimentation, or real-world deployment?
4. What evidence is being used to justify safety or usefulness?
5. Is the public framing running ahead of the regulatory framing?

## Why this matters for MedSentinel

The MHRA often appears in public discussion through:

- consultation reports
- sandbox initiatives
- innovation-friendly framing
- future-facing statements about safer or faster development

Those signals matter, but they should not be mistaken for proof that a product is clinically ready or broadly validated.

## Common mistake

Confusing a supportive innovation environment with resolved questions of evidence, safety, or deployment governance.

## What to look for in public claims

- sandbox announcements
- pilot language
- real-world testing claims
- references to patient benefit before clear evidence detail
- broad innovation framing with limited methodological detail
