# EU MDR and SaMD-Oriented Framing

Version: `v0.1`

## Why this matters

For health AI products operating in or discussed in Europe, EU MDR and SaMD-oriented thinking matter because software claims can quickly become device questions.

## What this document is for

This is not a legal summary of EU MDR.

It is a public research note for understanding the kinds of issues that arise when AI software is discussed in relation to medical-device style oversight.

## Key questions

1. Is the software performing a medical function?
2. Is it intended for diagnosis, screening, monitoring, prediction, or treatment support?
3. Is the AI framing being used as marketing language, or is it tied to a defined medical use?
4. What is the degree of clinician review or human oversight?
5. Does the product behave more like workflow software, or more like a medical decision-shaping system?

## Why this matters for MedSentinel

European health AI discussions often blur:

- digital health software
- workflow support
- clinical support
- device-like behavior

That ambiguity is exactly why intended use and practical influence matter so much.

## Common mistake

Assuming that software remains outside medical-device style scrutiny simply because it is framed as support, assistance, or efficiency.

## What to examine

- the exact clinical claim
- who the end user is
- whether outputs influence patient-specific decisions
- whether the system creates a meaningful downstream clinical effect
