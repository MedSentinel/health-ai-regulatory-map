# Glossary

Version: `v0.1`

This glossary is a lightweight orientation tool.
Definitions here are simplified for editorial and research use.
They should not be treated as formal legal interpretations.

## AI in healthcare

A broad umbrella term covering AI systems used in clinical, administrative, diagnostic, operational, or patient-facing healthcare contexts.

## SaMD

Software as a Medical Device.

Software that performs a medical function without being part of a hardware medical device.

## CDS

Clinical Decision Support.

Tools that support clinical work by surfacing, organizing, or suggesting information relevant to diagnosis, treatment, workflow, or care planning.

## Intended use

The specific purpose a manufacturer says a product is designed for.

This is often one of the most important concepts in regulatory interpretation.

## Patient-facing AI

AI systems that directly interact with patients or consumers, including symptom tools, assistants, screening interfaces, educational tools, and conversational systems.

## Medical advice boundary

The practical boundary between general health information and guidance that begins to function as individualized medical advice.

## Risk management

The structured process of identifying, assessing, mitigating, and monitoring risk across a product’s lifecycle.

## Human oversight

The degree to which a meaningful human can review, challenge, or interrupt the system before its output affects care or downstream action.

## Clinical validation

Evidence that a system performs acceptably for its intended medical use in a clinically relevant context.

## Regulatory status

The formal classification, authorization, clearance, or oversight relationship a product has with a regulator.

This is not the same thing as usefulness, trustworthiness, or safety.
